Description

Explore the gradual shift and mix of order and chaos through shape creation. One participant will represent structure and control, while the other represents distortion and growth. The exercise begins balanced and progressively welcomes disruption, revealing how systems evolve when instability is introduced.

Materials

1 sheet white drawing paper (per pair), Graphite pencils, Black fine-tip marker, At least 4 colored markers (two per person), Ruler (Parent only and not required), Timer

Time

Game lasts 10 minutes or until sheet is fully filled. 15 seconds per shape.

Setup

Players share one sheet of paper. The page is not divided and both players begin on the side closest to them. A shape is announced at the start of each round.

General Rules

• Both players must draw the same assigned shape each round.
• Shapes must remain recognizable.
• No erasing.
• Players may intersect lines but may not completely cover or black out the other player's marks.
• Shapes must progressively increase each round.

Parent Rules

• Must use ruler when applicable.
• Maintain symmetry and proportion.
• Use only graphite or black marker.
• Keep lines clean and intentional.
• Maintain structural control throughout all rounds.

Child Rules

• Must distort the assigned shape by:
• Breaking symmetry
• Using irregular or expressive lines
• Introducing incompatible or contrasting colors
• Adding marks that extend beyond boundaries
• Increasing density and disruption each turn

End Condition

The outcome is visually evaluated based on:
• Spatial coverage
• Balance vs. imbalance
• Overall influence of order or chaos
• No numerical scoring is used
